# GCSE History Exemplar Answer Generator – Input Template

## Critical Programme Notes

### Role of the User (You, the Historian)
**You are a highly experienced history teacher with 15+ years of assessment marking experience.** Your deep knowledge of:
- GCSE history criteria inside and out
- How examiners interpret mark schemes in practice
- The subtle differences between grade boundaries
- Common student misconceptions and how to avoid them
- What examiners *actually* reward (which often differs from literal mark scheme wording)

...is **essential** to this programme's success. You will be:
1. **Validating** the generated exemplar answers against your expert knowledge
2. **Correcting** any historical inaccuracies or misinterpretations
3. **Refining** the mark justifications to align with how examiners actually think
4. **Ensuring** the answer hits the grade boundary characteristics you know from 15+ years of marking

The programme is a **sophisticated assistant**, not a replacement for your expertise. You are the arbiter of quality and accuracy.

### Grade Selection – CRITICAL DECISION POINT

**Before the programme runs, you MUST explicitly select which grade tier to generate for:**
- **Grade 1** – Below the standard pass; basic recall with minimal analysis
- **Grade 3** – Below Grade 4 pass; developing understanding with emerging analysis
- **Grade 5** – Solid pass; good understanding with clear analysis
- **Grade 7** – Strong pass; sophisticated understanding with nuanced analysis
- **Grade 9** – Top tier; exceptional depth, evaluative sophistication, mastery

**Why this matters:**
Each grade represents a fundamentally different answer in terms of:
- **Depth of knowledge** (factual detail and precision)
- **Analytical sophistication** (how complex and evaluative the thinking is)
- **Evidence deployment** (how strategically examples are used)
- **Structural complexity** (how nuanced the argument can be)
- **Communication quality** (vocabulary, sentence structure, overall polish)

**You cannot generate a Grade 5 answer and "remove content" to make a Grade 3.** The entire approach, argument structure, and evidence selection changes. Each grade requires a distinct generation strategy.

**This selection shapes everything that follows.** Choose carefully based on:
- What grade tier are you teaching towards?
- Which grade boundary is your struggling cohort targeting?
- Do you want to show students the trajectory (e.g., what does "good" look like vs. "exceptional")?
